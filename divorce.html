A DIVORCE DECREE

  • by:Hamisi Yange
  • date:January 27, 2022

Divorce decree is a judicial judgment that terminates a marriage. It is where a marriage is declared to come to an end and all rights and duties of parties to a particular marriage stop. Divorce is one of the ways that terminates a marriage (other ways of terminating a marriage are termination by annulment or death). The valid divorce decree is supposed to be granted by a competent court.

For a divorce decree to be valid it must be issued by a competent court. According to Section 76 of the Law of Marriage Act, Cap 29 R.E 2019 the competent courts with original jurisdiction to entertain a divorce petition include High Court, a court of a resident magistrate, a district court and a primary court.

A person who wants a divorce decree has to file a divorce petition in either of the courts as Section 76 of the Law of Marriage Act, Cap 29 R.E 2019 provided. A divorce petition will be lodged in a primary court if parties were married in accordance with customary law or in Islamic form as provided under Section 75 of the Law of Marriage Act, Cap 29 R.E 2019.
